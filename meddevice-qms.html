Known & Unknown Factors

Known — Confirmed Signals
Regulatory complexity is high. FDA quality separation enforced. CAPA violations on record. The governance structure creates structured, AI-amenable data.
V&V throughput is the ceiling. Speed-to-clearance is the firm’s core competitive value. Rebuild loops are the friction point named unprompted.
Quality system is manual and people-dependent. One person runs it. Any growth, absence, or handoff is a risk.
Software team has coordination gaps. Young, technically strong, operationally immature. Needs structured workflow governance, not more engineers.
Business model is in transition. Contract grind → platform company → investor-ready. The transition is active, not aspirational.
Historical data archive exists. Years of bench test, predicate equivalence, and clinical data. Format and accessibility TBD.

Discovery — Four Focus Areas

Focus Area 01 — Regulatory
CAPA + Quality System
Walk us through a CAPA from trigger to close. Where does it live today?
When an FDA question comes back on a filing — how do you trace it to the source test or complaint?
Average CAPA cycle time? What’s the longest one you’ve had to manage?
Focus Area 02 — Validation
V&V Workflow + Cycle Time
On your most recent 510(k) — how many bench test cycles, and where did time disappear?
When an algorithm parameter changes — what triggers re-validation? Is that a judgment call or a defined rule?
How do you compare current device performance to your predicate today — manual or automated?
Focus Area 03 — Data
Archive + Historical Intelligence
How far back does the testing archive go? What format — spreadsheets, CSV, custom system?
Does a past engagement’s sanitized data exist that we could use to model a real demo?
Does historical test data get referenced when starting a new build, or does each project start fresh?
Focus Area 04 — Operations
Engineering + Program Management
How is the engineering team tracking tasks and milestones today — formal system or ad hoc?
If you could fix one operational thing in the next 90 days, what would it be?
What does “working on the business, not in the business” look like concretely for the firm right now?
