Operational Framework

The Dual-Flight Partner Integration Model

Two parallel tracks that must stay synchronized. Internal Flight is the platform's operational view — the certification and launch machinery. External Flight is the partner's lived experience — what they see, feel, and grade you on. The gap between the two views is where partner satisfaction goes to die. Stages tagged SDK are the new commerce tracks (Unified Account SSO + Shoppable TV API) that the standard cert pipeline does not yet cover.

The New Primitives

Commerce SDK — two integration tracks

The commerce thesis only pays off if partners can actually wire content to checkout. Two new SDK primitives sit outside the standard certification pipeline and get their own review track, test protocol, and QA matrix.

🔐

Unified Account SSO

Single sign-on that ties the living-room session to the retail-parent account — the authentication layer that makes closed-loop attribution possible.

  • Account-state handling across app and platform
  • Opt-in / opt-out and data-permissioning compliance
  • Attribution coverage from exposure to verified purchase
🛒

Shoppable TV API

The on-screen commerce primitive — QR and remote-triggered shoppable units that turn a branded content moment into a cart without leaving the couch.

  • Purchase-flow interruption + cart-state persistence
  • QR / remote shoppable-unit trigger reliability
  • Product-link validation against the retail storefront
Automation Strategy

The AI & Automation Integration Layer

Not AI for its own sake — compressing the human-in-the-loop bottlenecks that slow partner time-to-live. Each node maps to a specific friction point in the certification and account-management pipeline.

🔍Automated Pre-Cert Screening

Static analysis of submitted build artifacts against known failure patterns and platform spec. Catches ~60–70% of common rejection causes before human QA — cutting clean-submission cert cycles from 15 → 8 days.

Leverage · High
💬Intelligent Onboarding Assistant

An LLM assistant trained on platform developer docs and common partner-ops responses. Surfaces contextually relevant docs by app category (SVOD vs. FAST vs. sports). Target: 40% reduction in Tier 2/3 interrupt rate.

Leverage · High
⚠️QA Anomaly Detection

An ML model on historical QA logs flags statistical anomalies in device performance (memory, load time, crash rate) vs. expected baseline by app category — surfacing regression risk before the certification gate.

Leverage · High
📊Partner Health Scoring

A health index computed from engagement trends, ticket velocity, QBR participation, revenue trajectory, and integration depth — surfacing early churn or growth signals to drive partner-ops prioritization.

Leverage · Med-High
📋Certification Status Portal

A real-time, partner-facing dashboard showing submission state, feedback reports, and cert-cycle position — replacing status-chasing email threads with self-serve visibility.

Leverage · Med-High
📈Automated Performance Narratives

Monthly partner analytics auto-generated with plain-language insight ("retention improved X% vs. platform average") — turning a raw dashboard into a strategy conversation.
