Core Positioning
AI-Powered Regulatory + Operational Intelligence Layer
Haku operates at a rare intersection — the technical depth of a research lab, the speed of a startup, and the regulatory fluency of a firm that has been through the FDA process and come out the other side with clearances. The opportunity is to build infrastructure that protects and amplifies that expertise — so the team can work on the business, not just in it, and so the institutional knowledge that makes Haku exceptional becomes a durable, scalable asset.

Operational Pain Zones — What We’re Solving

Zone 01 — V&V Throughput
Rebuild & Retest Loops
Every algorithm change triggers full revalidation. Engineers become regression machines. Speed-to-clearance is Haku’s competitive edge — V&V cycle time is the ceiling on how many clients they can run. Unprompted signal: “V&D testing, shit like that.”
Zone 02 — Quality System
Manual CAPA & DHF Operations
One person owns the entire quality system. Every CAPA, every DHF entry, every compliance audit trail — manually managed. FDA already enforces quality independence. The Philips CPAP story is the baseline risk they navigate daily. “I run the whole quality system, which could be kind of tough.”
Zone 03 — Data Architecture
Archive as Dead Weight
Years of bench test results, predicate equivalence data, clinical outcomes — siloed, manual, or buried. The archive should be an active intelligence asset, not a reference library. Every new build should benefit from every past engagement. Confirmed signal: historical data concept resonated immediately.
Zone 04 — Operational Maturity
Founder Dependency & Scale Ceiling
Haku operates at enterprise complexity on 10 people. The expertise that makes them exceptional lives in two people’s heads. That’s not a team problem — it’s an infrastructure problem. The transition from services company to platform company requires a different kind of foundation.
