Why Does This Matter?

  • Lost revenue sits idle. Every day a charger is offline, a revenue-sharing stream that your property is entitled to goes uncollected. Over 120 days, that represents a meaningful financial impact that can be restored with relatively simple action.
  • Your community is underserved. EV drivers — your residents, guests, or the public depending on the site — have lost access to infrastructure they rely on. This can affect satisfaction, retention, and your property's competitive position in the market.
  • California's EV mandate is accelerating. With over 40% of new vehicle sales trending toward EVs statewide, charging availability is rapidly becoming a baseline amenity expectation — not a premium feature.
  • Inaction compounds the problem. Hardware left offline can degrade further from weather exposure, vandalism risk, or software obsolescence, increasing the cost and complexity of eventual recovery.
🎯

What You Can Do About It

  • Complete the Site Host Information Form. This is the single most impactful action you can take right now. It takes 5 minutes and starts the evaluation with no commitment required.
  • Locate your unit's serial number. Usually found on a label affixed to the charger hardware. Having this ready speeds up the diagnostic phase significantly — if you can't locate it, no worries, our team can help track it down for you.
  • Flag this to your property ownership or asset management team. Decisions about network agreements and revenue sharing may require sign-off above the facilities level — the sooner the right people are looped in, the faster service can resume.
  • Schedule a call. If you'd prefer to talk it through before submitting anything, reach Oz Rosario directly at (619) 987-4040 or oz.rosario@gmail.com. No pressure, no sales pitch — just straight answers.
🔋

The Long-Term Solution

  • A network built for stability. It's a California-registered, financially stable network operator. Unlike smaller operators that may exit the market or go insolvent, the charging network's infrastructure is designed for long-term, continuous operation with defined SLA commitments.
  • Proactive monitoring — not reactive firefighting. Our network operations center monitors charger uptime 24/7. If a unit goes offline, our team is alerted and a response is initiated before most site hosts even notice — reducing or eliminating disruptions.
  • Full operational accountability. Every site host receives monthly performance reports with uptime metrics, session counts, energy delivered, and revenue breakdown. Transparency is built into the model — you'll always know exactly how your chargers are performing.
  • AMPPED as your local advocate. As the California program lead, AMPPED is your dedicated point of contact throughout and beyond the reactivation process — providing local presence, rapid response, and ongoing relationship support that national operators simply cannot match.
