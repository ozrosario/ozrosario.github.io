SHT 01

The Thesis: Staff the Role. Earn the Build.

The firm has budgeted for an Accounting & Administrative Support hire. This plan fills that role for less than a traditional employee actually costs — and uses the role itself as the discovery engine for an Agentic Operating System that the firm owns outright.

The firm is a California-based civil and environmental engineering firm with national project experience: $250M+ in total constructed value, $250M+ in client funding secured, and 200+ completed projects, with deep public works exposure that makes California prevailing wage (DIR) compliance a live, recurring operational burden. The back office supporting that work runs on QuickBooks Online, Microsoft 365, Teams, and SharePoint — held together by manual process, spreadsheets, and institutional memory.

A sizing note that matters. The $250M figures are cumulative constructed value and funding secured across the firm's history — not annual revenue. The firm operates as a lean, sophisticated firm (roughly 25–35 people). Every cost, ROI, and staffing assumption in this plan is calibrated to that reality, not to an enterprise. The right solution is a compliance-aware operating layer sized for a firm this size — not an enterprise transformation.

The conventional move is to hire an admin/accounting specialist and absorb the manual work. This plan proposes a different sequence, in three commitments — each gated by evidence from the one before it:

1. The role funds the discovery. An embedded B2B specialist performs the full accounting & admin function at a cost below the fully-loaded cost of the budgeted hire. While doing the work, every workflow gets documented, timed, and baselined.

2. The discovery de-risks the build. At Day 60, the firm receives a data-backed proposal for an Agentic OS layer — grounded in measured hours, error rates, and friction points from inside its own operation. Not vendor projections. The firm's own numbers.

3. The build pays for itself out of measured savings. Automation pilots launch only where evidence supports them, with human review gates on every action and the firm retaining permanent ownership of everything built.

At no point does the firm take a leap of faith larger than one month's invoice. That gating is not just elegant — it is necessary: no firm this size signs a six-figure AI build cold. The role-funded discovery is what makes a serious build digestible.

Why gated? Most AI engagements ask the client to fund a build on projections. The "before" picture cannot be disputed — it was lived, not estimated.

SHT 04

The Platform: One Operating Layer, Not Ten Bots

The most common — and most expensive — mistake is to build ten disconnected AI agents. The right design is one operating layer that the ten agent workflows sit on top of: a single data model, one rules engine, one human-review queue, one audit trail. Build the foundation once; expose capabilities on top of it.

Fig. 04-A — Layered architecture (shared infrastructure, then agent workflows)
LayerPurpose
1 · IntakePulls data from SharePoint, Excel, email, QuickBooks Online, timekeeping/payroll, COIs, and project folders
2 · Document IntelligenceOCR / vision layer (e.g., Azure Document Intelligence) extracts project names, vendors, wage classifications, invoices, receipts, COIs, and expiration dates from unstructured PDFs into structured data
3 · Project Data ModelCanonical records for project, client, contract, employee, subconsultant, invoice, pay period, and funding source
4 · Rules EngineApplies project rules, DIR / prevailing-wage flags, approval thresholds, insurance requirements, and billing logic — deterministically
5 · Agent OrchestrationRoutes each task to the correct agent workflow with role-based permissions
6 · HITL Work QueuePresents exceptions and approvals to management, PMs, accounting, or a junior operations associate
7 · Audit & ReportingMaintains logs, evidence trails, reconciliation reports, dashboards, and export packages

The non-negotiable design rule — the "deterministic sandbox." An AI model must never calculate a prevailing wage, an apprentice ratio, or a fringe benefit. On a public works project a single wrong wage tier is a zero-tolerance compliance event with real financial and legal exposure. The model's only job is to read and extract (hours from a timesheet, a classification from a contract). The math is then handed to a hard-coded, versioned, human-auditable rules engine. Understanding is AI; calculation is deterministic code. This separation is the heart of doing this safely for an engineering firm with public works exposure.

Because the firm already runs QuickBooks Online (not the desktop edition), the financial integration uses native OAuth REST APIs rather than a brittle on-premise bridge — removing roughly 20–30% of the build complexity and the need for dedicated middleware. The unstructured-document pipeline (Layer 2) remains the real engineering lift, because the operational truth currently lives in SharePoint PDFs and Excel files.

SHT 05

The First 90 Days: Listen, Map, Pilot

Guiding principle: before automating anything, understand how it actually works. Automation proposals come only after operational credibility is earned. The 90-day window governs the role and discovery — the Agentic OS build runs on its own gated, sprint-based timeline and is not bound to this calendar.

Days 1–30 · Listen, Learn, Deliver

Orient. Perform. Build trust.

Shadow and execute existing workflows end-to-end — QuickBooks Online configuration, timesheet routing, payroll schedule, invoice formats, prevailing wage / certified payroll tracking. Meet every stakeholder; identify who is most process-burdened. Inventory all file systems (Teams, SharePoint, local), naming conventions, and version control. Map the certificate-of-insurance tracking process and its gaps.

Day 30 DeliverableInternal operations snapshot — current state, workflow dependencies, initial observations.
Days 31–60 · Map, Model, Propose

Audit workflows. Draft the architecture. Validate before building.

Catalog tasks by frequency, time cost, and error rate; score each for automation readiness. Sketch the layered platform — intake, document intelligence, data model, rules engine, orchestration, HITL queue. Map the tool stack to existing the firm infrastructure: QuickBooks Online API, Microsoft Graph / SharePoint, document AI, email classification. Present to leadership; refine scope to what is achievable and genuinely useful.

Day 60 Deliverable · Layer 2 GateAgentic OS Proposal: workflow priority matrix, layered architecture, tooling requirements, measured manual-hour savings per workflow — and the build SOW, priced against the firm's own data.
Days 61–90 · Build, Pilot, Document

Two pilots. Full playbooks. The firm owns everything.

Pilot 1 — Insurance tracking: automated COI monitor that ingests certificates, extracts expiration dates, flags renewals 30 days out, and drafts follow-up emails to subs and brokers. Pilot 2 — Invoice drafting: agent pulls timesheet and expense data, populates invoice templates, routes drafts to the PM for single-click review. Every automation includes a human review gate. Each pilot ships with a full playbook: trigger conditions, agent logic, failure modes, override procedures, and a handoff protocol.

Day 90 DeliverableImplementation Playbook v1.0 — live pilots, performance baseline (hours saved, error reduction), expansion roadmap.
SHT 06

The Agent Stack & Build Sequencing

The full Agentic OS spans ten agent workflows across accounting and administrative support. They deploy in sequenced sprints set by the Day-60 audit — not by ambition — with the lowest-risk, highest-repetition work first and the compliance-critical work last, after the system has earned trust.

Fig. 06-A — Build sequence: three sprints, de-risked
SprintAgentsWhy this order
Sprint 1 — Quick winsAR/AP (03), Meetings (08), Correspondence (09)Pure LLM + QuickBooks / Microsoft Graph API plays. Easy to build, immediate time savings, low risk. Builds momentum and trust.
Sprint 2 — Data heavy liftRecordkeeping (04), Insurance/COI (07), File Systems (10)Requires the document-intelligence (OCR) pipeline to ingest SharePoint PDFs. The real engineering investment, but still low compliance risk.
Sprint 3 — Compliance tierPayroll (01), Prevailing Wage (02), Audit (05)The deterministic rules engine for CA DIR comes last. Only after the system has proven it handles standard documents and accounting do you introduce zero-tolerance compliance math.

Sequencing is a risk control, not just a schedule. Prevailing wage and certified payroll are the highest-stakes workflows in the firm. Automating them first would mean betting trust you haven't yet earned on the workflow least tolerant of error. Doing them last means the rules engine ships into a system that has already demonstrated reliability — and into a team that already trusts the human-review gates.

SHT 08

Operating Leverage: Phase A → B → C

The strategic payoff is not Year-1 savings — it is what happens to the cost curve as project volume grows. A human-staffed back office scales almost linearly. An agentic back office scales sublinearly, because the data model, rules engine, and document pipelines are reused.

Phase A — Today (the senior operator as bottleneck)

One senior generalist performs the consolidated PM / office / accounting function manually, while also driving any systems work. Capacity is capped by hours; every new project adds linear administrative load — more certified payroll runs, more COIs to chase, more invoices to assemble. Operational truth lives in SharePoint PDFs, Excel, and people's heads, which means low audit readiness and total key-person risk. Exact baseline hours and error rates are captured live during Days 1–60; the figures here update with measured data at the Day-60 gate.

Phase B — Agentic OS live (agents handle 75–90% of repetitive volume)

The system drafts, reconciles, routes, files, flags, summarizes, and reports; humans hold the review gates. The residual HITL burden — confirmations, exception handling, relationship touchpoints — is structurally absorbable by a junior operations associate at a far lower fully-loaded cost than the senior role. Senior capacity redirects to higher-leverage work: project delivery support, the next funding round, growth.

SHT 10

Governance: Trust Before Autonomy. Ownership Always.

Principle 01

Human review on every action

No agent sends, files, pays, or invoices without staff confirmation. Autonomy is granted gradually, per-workflow, only after a sustained accuracy record — and can be revoked instantly.

Principle 02

Compliance-sensitive workflows last

Prevailing wage and certified payroll deploy only after lower-risk agents prove the pattern, and only through the deterministic rules engine. Public works exposure is a hard constraint, not an optimization target.

Principle 03

The firm owns everything

Every agent ships with a full playbook: trigger conditions, logic, failure modes, override procedures, and a departure-proof handoff. The goal is organizational capability — not dependency on any consultant or vendor.

Data security follows the firm's existing Microsoft 365 perimeter — agents operate inside the tenant wherever possible, with role-based access control (payroll PII restricted from correspondence workflows), encryption for sensitive project and payroll data, and audit logs on every agent decision. The AgOS prepares, validates, and packages data for compliance systems like DIR and LCPtracker where those are required; it does not replace them.

1 · Fee Architecture & Margin Logic

Ask $7.5–8K/month on a 1099 / B2B structure; lead with the fully-loaded math; do not negotiate against yourself. At $8K you are still $9–23K cheaper than the budgeted hire; at $7.5K, $15–29K cheaper. Negotiation floor: $7K/mo. Below that, the discovery work is being given away. If pushed under $7.5K, trade price for term (6-month initial commitment) or for an explicit Day-60 review-meeting commitment from the decision maker.
