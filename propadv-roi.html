Bonus Outcome — Insurance Product Recovery

Improving the 2% Insurance
Take Rate.

The in-house insurance product launched with a 2% take rate — well below viable commercial thresholds. This is not a product failure. It's a distribution and data problem — both of which are solvable through the AI agent operating system and agentic workflow tooling being built as part of Pillar 2.

Root Cause Analysis

Why 2%? — Three Solvable Problems
  • No behavioral targeting: Insurance was offered uniformly without resident risk profiling or propensity scoring
  • No timing optimization: Offer presented at move-in (lowest trust moment) rather than at engagement peaks
  • No social proof loop: No community-level adoption visibility to trigger FOMO or peer influence
  • No personalization: Generic offer language — not unit-specific, not resident-profile-matched
  • No follow-up sequence: Single-touch offer with no nurture workflow or objection-handling automation

Agentic Solution Path — Pillar 2

How the AI OS Lifts Take Rate to 15–25%
  • Resident risk profiling agent: Score residents by unit, lease history, and payment behavior to identify high-propensity targets
  • Trigger-based offer timing: Send insurance offers at renewal, post-maintenance request, and after community incident — when insurance value is most salient
  • Personalized offer copy agent: Generate unit-specific, resident-profile-matched offer language at scale through the VisLogic.AI OS
  • Community adoption dashboard: Show boards and PMs real-time take rate progress — creates internal accountability and visibility
  • Automated nurture sequence: 3-touch follow-up workflow with objection handlers — mimicking the sales playbook methodology from Pillar 1
