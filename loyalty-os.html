00

The thesis: earn the renewal

VIP's real moat was never the 10% off. It's forty years of community equity with runners and walkers, a fit-and-service experience no online seller can copy, and a paid membership at real scale. The strategic move is to stop competing on discount — which anyone can match — and start being the membership people renew on purpose.

"VIP saves my family money, helps us get the right fit, and keeps us moving."

The one sentence a member should be able to say about the program.

Why "Movement Membership," and why now

The market has shifted. Members increasingly value access, utility, recognition, community, and service over one more point of cash back. The strongest programs combine an economic engine with identity and participation. VIP has the economic engine already; the work is to add the human and community layers on top of it — and to make every member feel the value clearly enough that renewal becomes a choice, not a surprise.

ProtectThe hard value: 10% everyday savings, Rewards Cash, the 90-Day Perfect Fit Guarantee, free shipping, family sharing. These built the program. Don't break the P&L chasing shiny objects.
ChangeMake the renewal and enrollment experience transparent and easy — the program earns the renewal. Re-market access ("First Dibs") as a headline benefit. Make tiers differ in kind, not just percentage.
BuildOne member identity across store, app, and web. A community and partner layer. A fit profile and shoe-rotation guidance that make the membership useful between purchases.
The mantra: Earn the Right A program — and the associate pitch on the floor — should earn the member's renewal, never extract it. Transparent value, clear benefits, easy choices. "Earn the Right" is both the operating principle and the language store teams already understand.
01

The 2026 Movement Membership

Keep the economic engine. Add a free on-ramp, a non-cash way to earn status for moving, a genuinely useful app, and tiers that mean something. The existing VIP name has equity — protect it, and make clear it stands for movement support, not just footwear savings.

Membership architecture

LayerPositionWhat it includes
Member (Free)The on-rampDigital account, receipt history, fit profile, store & event discovery, basic challenges, saved products. Captures identity and becomes the conversion funnel to paid VIP. Does not get the signature paid benefits.
VIP FamilyEveryday savings & confidenceThe existing core: 10% everyday savings, 5% Rewards Cash, 90-Day Perfect Fit Guarantee, family profiles, free shipping, fit history.
VIP PlusMore movement, more supportHigher Rewards Cash plus priority fit appointments, advanced rotation guidance, enhanced event access, brand-launch reservations, a family guest pass.
VIP PlatinumPremium expertise & recognitionTop Rewards Cash plus premium Fit Expert access, an annual biomechanics/RunDNA-style session where available, premium launch & demo access, select race-entry credits, surprise-and-delight.

Differentiate the upper tiers by access, service, and experience — not only by a deeper cash-back number. That protects margin while raising perceived value. A future option: let members either pay the fee or earn the tier through qualifying spend and engagement.

Two currencies, one membership

Rewards Cash — keep it simple

Stays financial and transparent: shown in dollars, visible in app, on the receipt, and to associates. No mystery formulas, clear expiration, partner-funded amounts tagged separately.

Move Status — the new layer

A non-cash currency earned for valuable engagement: fit appointments, store check-ins, completing a first walk or run, event attendance, reviews, referrals, updating shoe mileage, trade-ins. Unlocks recognition, access, and experiences — not another discount. This is what raises engagement without eroding margin.

The app: a movement dashboard, not a catalog with push

VIP Wallet

Tier, renewal date, Rewards Cash balance, recent earnings, expiring value, and a running tally of value received this year.

Fit Passport

Foot-scan history, size by brand/model, Fit Expert notes, insole and support preferences, prior return reasons — for each household member.

Shoe Garage

Shoes in rotation, purchase date, use case, optional mileage, expected replacement window, and "compare the next generation."

Store Mode

Digital membership card, appointment check-in, BOPIS status, inventory locator, associate-assisted cart.

In-store: the moat (≈60% of the work)

The store is where VIP wins and where most of the experience lives. The associate should see a concise service brief — not a customer's whole history — that makes the conversation personal: status and renewal date, household, Rewards Cash, previous shoes and sizes, a fit summary, stated goals, recent returns, and a recommended next action. Every footwear pickup should offer a two-minute fit confirmation, a Shoe Garage add, and a no-pressure reminder of the Perfect Fit Guarantee.

Community & partners: nationally enabled, locally run

Productize local running culture into a national benefit: beginner run/walk groups, "back to movement" programs, family and women's events, race-course previews, product-demo runs. The retailer's structural advantage is that it can host many brands' communities under one roof — something no single brand can do. Brands, races, recovery and training partners can co-fund experiences, reducing direct cost while making the membership feel premium.

02

How to run the program

Three numbers matter most: joins, renewal, and frequency. Everything else is an input. Protect the engine, fix the trust and emotional gaps, build the infrastructure that lets the program grow without buying loyalty by the discount point.

Protect

  • The 10% everyday-savings promise — easy to understand, immediate value. Don't destabilize it before understanding margin by segment.
  • The 90-Day Perfect Fit Guarantee — one of the strongest trust assets in retail, and on-mission for a company built on pain-free movement.
  • Family sharing — convert it from a billing feature into a true household-engagement model.
  • Fit expertise and the human, founder-rooted feel. The program should stay personal, not become an anonymous tech platform.

Change

  • Make renewal a trust advantage. Plain-language disclosure at sign-up; an immediate confirmation showing price, term, and how to cancel; pre-renewal reminders; easy online cancellation; a personalized annual value statement. The message: members stay because VIP earns the renewal.
  • Simplify the tier story to one sentence each — Family: everyday savings and confidence; Plus: more access and support; Platinum: premium expertise and experiences.
  • Rebalance upper-tier value toward service, access, and partner-funded experiences instead of ever-deeper cash-back liability.
  • Change store incentives from join-count alone to a balanced scorecard: qualified joins, 30-day activation, benefit comprehension, renewal, and satisfaction.
  • Make value visible at every touch: what did I earn, what can I use now, what am I close to unlocking, what value have I received this year, when do I renew.

Build

  • A free Member identity layer and a clean, single member ID across store, app, and web.
  • VIP Wallet, Fit Passport, Shoe Garage, and household profiles.
  • A community-event platform and a partner-reward marketplace.
  • An experimentation framework with holdouts, and an incremental-margin view of the program — not member-vs-nonmember vanity comparisons.

Operating rhythm

Weekly — Growth Room

Loyalty, CRM, E-comm, Retail Ops, Analytics, Care, Product. KPI movement, experiment readouts, member friction, store feedback, next launches.

Monthly — Business Review

Membership P&L, joins and acquisition cost, frequency, benefit utilization, renewal and churn, reward liability, incremental margin, test portfolio.

Quarterly — VIP Council

With the founder and executives: benefit and pricing decisions, tier design, roadmap, partnerships, store changes, growth goals.

North Star

Incremental contribution margin per active VIP household — paired with a customer North Star: the share of active households that receive and recognize meaningful value each quarter.

03

The AI layer

AI's job here is to make the human expert more prepared, the member better understood, and the next interaction more relevant — never to impersonate expertise or replace the Fit Expert. Used this way, it strengthens the exact thing that makes the retailer different. Each of these can be prototyped and piloted quickly, then hardened.

CLIENTELING
The pre-appointment service brief

Before a member walks in or arrives for a fit, generate a one-paragraph brief for the associate: last shoes and sizes, insole preferences, prior fit issues or returns, training goal, estimated shoe mileage, and a suggested next action. The associate stays fully accountable for the recommendation — they just start the conversation already knowing the member.

PREDICTION
Shoe-rotation & replacement modeling

Estimate when a member's shoes are wearing out from purchase date, use case, and optional connected-activity data, and trigger a right-time, helpful nudge ("your trainers are likely past their best — here's what's new in your fit"). Replaces generic blast replenishment with timely, trusted guidance.

RETENTION
Renewal-risk scoring & proactive save

Identify members drifting toward a lapse — low engagement, unused rewards, no recent visit — and route them into a value-first save journey well before renewal. This is "Earn the Right" operationalized: reach members with a reason to stay before the renewal date, not a surprise charge after it.

ORCHESTRATION
Lifecycle journeys, automated and reliable

Move from batch email to triggered, behavior-based journeys — onboarding, first-90-days activation, replacement-window nudges, renewal runway, win-back. The renewal-reminder and member-communication workflows run automatically and consistently, so transparency isn't dependent on someone remembering to send it.

PERSONALIZATION
Fit-grounded recommendations

Recommendations anchored in the member's actual fit profile and history rather than generic best-sellers — including knowing when a fit refresh in-store beats a reorder, and surfacing category gaps like recovery footwear or socks.

OPERATIONS
Agentic back-office workflows

The unglamorous plumbing — document handling, partner-reward reconciliation, identity de-duplication, compliance and communication logging — can be automated with agentic workflows, freeing the team to spend time on members. A loyalty leader who can prototype these directly, not just brief a vendor, compresses the time from idea to running system.

GuardrailAny biomechanics or fit output is framed as retail guidance, not medical diagnosis. AI prepares and supports; the human expert decides. Member data is used with clear consent, purpose limitation, and easy controls.
04

Pilots — test, measure, learn

Eight pilots, each with a hypothesis, a control, a clear success bar, and a scale-or-kill decision. Sized to be manageable for local store teams. Tap to expand.

P-01Fit Passport›
Hypothesis
A persistent, member-accessible fit record increases footwear conversion, reduces avoidable returns, and improves repeat purchase.
Design
Eight test stores vs. eight matched controls; associates save scan, products tried, size, insoles, and notes to the profile; member gets an app summary and a 14-day follow-up.
Success
Fit-session-to-purchase conversion, return rate, 90-day repeat, Passport activation, associate adoption.
Timeline
8 weeks.
P-02Shoe Garage & rotation coach›
Hypothesis
Timely, use-based replacement guidance outperforms generic replenishment emails on frequency and trust.
Design
Members add shoes manually or from history, optionally connect activity; system sends rotation-health, replacement-window, and fit-refresh prompts vs. matched control.
Success
Garage opt-in, replenishment conversion, days between purchases, return rate, perceived helpfulness.
Timeline
10–12 weeks.
P-03Run Local, Earn Local›
Hypothesis
Repeated local participation builds emotional loyalty and store frequency more efficiently than an equivalent discount.
Design
Six stores host recurring run/walk formats (beginner, walking, family); a non-cash status badge after the first visit, a partner-funded reward after the third.
Success
Repeat attendance, store visits, incremental spend, renewal intent, event NPS.
Timeline
12 weeks.
P-04Renew with Confidence›
Hypothesis
Transparent reminders, a clear value statement, and frictionless choice increase intentional renewal while reducing complaints.
Design
A/B the current renewal journey vs. a sequence: 30-day notice, 7-day reminder, annual value summary, easy renew/downgrade/cancel, optional feedback, recovery outreach for the dissatisfied.
Success
Intentional renewal, save rate, support contacts, post-renewal activity and satisfaction.
Timeline
Two renewal cycles (~8–12 weeks).
P-05Family Relay Referral›
Hypothesis
Members acquire higher-quality members at lower cost when the reward is tied to a completed first purchase, not a sign-up.
Design
Invite up to three; referrer rewarded only after the referred member's qualified purchase clears the return window; test cash vs. access vs. Move Status as the incentive.
Success
Referred join rate, CAC vs. paid acquisition, 90-day repeat, household expansion.
Timeline
8 weeks.
P-06Brand Launch Pass›
Hypothesis
Brand-funded access and discovery differentiates more than another discount and drives new-to-brand sales.
Design
Partner with Brooks, Hoka, or On for a limited launch: VIP reservation window, local demo run, Fit Expert education, partner-funded experience reward.
Success
Reservations, attendance, launch conversion, new-to-brand customers, partner funding and reinvestment.
Timeline
6 weeks per launch.
P-07Second Lap Trade-In›
Hypothesis
Footwear trade-in, donation, or recycling reactivates lapsed members and creates purchase urgency — and the retailer can do it across all brands.
Design
Four to six stores accept eligible footwear via an operating partner; higher credit for reusable, smaller for recyclable; a 30-day bonus for using the credit on a replacement.
Success
Take-back volume, reactivation, credit redemption, replacement-purchase margin, member sentiment.
Timeline
12–16 weeks.
P-08BOPIS Finish Line›
Hypothesis
A short, optional fit confirmation at pickup increases confidence and attachment without materially adding labor.
Design
At pickup, offer immediate handoff, a two-minute fit check, a scheduled 10-minute appointment, a prepared alternate size, and a Shoe Garage add; one permissioned geofenced reminder on approach.
Success
Fit-check acceptance, add-on sales, return rate, satisfaction, incremental labor under two minutes.
Timeline
8 weeks.

Test discipline: every pilot has a control, a pre-set success bar, and a scale/kill gate. Throughput of learning beats the cleverness of any single test.
