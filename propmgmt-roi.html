Model Assumptions

How This Model Is Built —
And Why It Holds Up.

The projections in this model are designed to be conservative, defensible, and grounded in how managed services program economics actually work in residential property management. The key distinction throughout: the firm is the management company and program operator — not the property owner. PA's direct revenue is modeled accordingly.

Revenue Participant Structure

Each infrastructure vertical generates gross ecosystem revenue split across four parties: Service Operator (PA's white-label managed services division), HOA Community (the collective ownership body), Vendor/Provider (the infrastructure operator), and PA Program Management (earned facilitation fee). The Service Operator designation reflects a future-state structure where the firm operates managed services verticals under its own brand — similar in model to the in-house insurance product, but designed for infrastructure.

PA's Direct Revenue Mechanism

the firm's direct earned income from this program flows through two sources: (1) a 15% program management fee on the total gross vertical revenue generated per property — industry standard for managed services facilitation at PM firms operating infrastructure programs — and (2) a management contract repricing uplift of $6/unit/year, modeled conservatively once infrastructure verticals are live and delivering measurable community value.

Portfolio Scale & Activation Realism

The full portfolio baseline is modeled at 100 HOAs × 130 avg. units = 13,000 units, excluding 2025–2026 acquisitions. Activation projections are deliberately throttled to reflect real HOA adoption dynamics: boards are slow to move, decisions require community votes, and this program requires a dedicated sales driver. 25% portfolio activation by end of 2026 is the reach goal — not 50%. Scale projections above 50% are shown as multi-year targets, not near-term commitments.

Model Assumptions — Applied Throughout
  • PA direct revenue = Program Management Fee at 15% of total gross vertical revenue per property — reflects industry standard for infrastructure program facilitation at residential PM firms operating white-label managed services
  • PA additional revenue = Management Contract Uplift at $6/unit/year — conservative repricing premium once infrastructure verticals are active, demonstrable, and delivering measurable NOI to the community; modeled as $0.50/unit/month
  • Revenue splits by vertical use midpoint values from the Revenue Participation Model: EV Charging — Service Operator 25%, HOA 7.5%; Solar/BESS — Service Operator 45%, HOA 5%; Telecom TEM — Service Operator 60%, HOA 0%; Connectivity — Service Operator 50%, HOA 15%; IoT SaaS — Service Operator 30%, HOA 20%
  • HOA share flows through the HOA board's independent governance process — PA does not capture HOA revenue directly, but earns on the program facilitation that makes it possible
  • Asset value uplift from NOI growth accrues to property owners and HOA communities — PA's benefit is indirect through mandate retention, enterprise valuation re-rate, and competitive positioning
  • Activation projections: Reach goal 25% by end of 2026; 35–40% by mid-2027; 40–50% by end of 2027 — calibrated to single-driver sales velocity with HOA board adoption realities
  • To accelerate toward the upper activation range, a dedicated sales team of 1–2 reps would be required to expand portfolio penetration velocity

Activation Velocity — A Candid Note

HOA boards are notoriously deliberate. Each activation requires a board presentation, a community vote, vendor coordination, and contract execution. With a single program driver, reaching 15% by end of 2026 is a realistic operational target — 25% is a stretch goal that requires everything to go right. Reaching 35–50% by end of 2027 is achievable, but meaningfully accelerated by adding 1–2 dedicated sales representatives to the program. That decision point is worth a separate discussion.

Scale Narrative

The 3-property proof of concept generates $34,317 in direct PA income annually — $88 per unit. That's the unit economics. What makes this engagement compelling is the portfolio behind it: 100+ HOAs, 13,000+ units. At 15% activation by end of 2026, the firm is generating over $171K per year in new program income. At the 2026 reach goal of 25% activation, that grows to nearly $286K annually. At 50% activation — which requires a sales team and takes through end of 2027 — the program generates $572K per year. These are defensible, grounded projections. Not a ceiling — a floor for what gets built with the right execution.

The $4.2M —
Who It Belongs To, and Why PA Still Benefits.

The aggregate asset value uplift from NOI growth accrues primarily to property owners and HOA communities — not directly to the firm. That's the point of the model. PA's benefit from this value creation is indirect, structural, and meaningful — and it compounds over time.

How the firm Benefits Indirectly — Three Mechanisms

Mechanism 1 — Mandate Retention
Higher community value = stickier management contracts

When a management company demonstrably increases a community's asset value by $477K–$2.2M, the HOA board has a quantified reason to renew and expand the management contract. Churn cost drops and renewal pricing improves — a real financial benefit even if it doesn't show up as direct revenue.

Conservative value at 25% activation: $8K–$20K/yr in reduced re-acquisition cost
Mechanism 2 — Enterprise Valuation Re-Rate
Managed services income commands a higher multiple

Traditional property management firms trade at 4–6× EBITDA. Firms with managed services recurring revenue streams — especially infrastructure-linked — trade at 7–12× EBITDA. Adding $171K–$286K in new managed services income by end of 2026 directly re-rates enterprise value at a meaningful spread.

At 25% activation + 8× vs. 5× on $286K new EBITDA: approximately +$857K on enterprise value by end of 2026
Mechanism 3 — Competitive Mandate Wins
Infrastructure value prop wins new management contracts

A management company that can demonstrably add $88/unit/year in new income to a community — with zero capital required from the HOA — wins RFPs against competitors who cannot make that offer. The pitch shifts from "we manage your community" to "we monetize it." That's a differentiated competitive position.

Each new HOA won at 130 avg. units: $11,440/yr new PA program income. Two incremental wins per year = $22,880 compounding annually

Framing the Opportunity

The $213K figure from the Strategic Plan represents the total gross NOI the ecosystem generates across all participants — the communities, the service operators, and the vendors. The firm's direct program income is a different number: approximately $88 per unit per year through program management fees and contract repricing. On the 3 reference properties, that's $34K. The real story is what that unit rate becomes at portfolio scale. the firm manages over 100 HOAs. At a realistic 15% activation by end of 2026, the program generates $171K per year in new direct income. At the 2026 reach goal of 25%, that grows to nearly $286K annually. The $75,000 engagement investment is recovered in approximately five months at that run rate. The asset value uplift — $65–70M across 25% of the portfolio — belongs to the communities the firm serves. That's the proof of value that wins mandates and keeps them.
